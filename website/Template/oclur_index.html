Overview

Ocular diseases encompass a range of conditions affecting the eyes, including cataracts and glaucoma, which can lead to vision impairment and blindness. Cataracts involve the clouding of the eye's lens, causing blurred vision, while glaucoma, characterized by increased intraocular pressure, can lead to optic nerve damage. Age-related macular degeneration affects the central vision, diabetic retinopathy results from diabetes-related vascular damage, and conjunctivitis causes eye inflammation. Early diagnosis and appropriate treatment are vital for maintaining visual function and preventing irreversible complications in ocular diseases. Regular eye check-ups with healthcare professionals contribute to the effective management of these conditions and overall eye wellness..

  • Eye pain or discomfort may be indicative of conditions like glaucoma or conjunctivitis. Pain can range from a mild irritation to severe, sharp discomfort.
  • Visual distortions, such as straight lines appearing wavy or distorted, are often associated with age-related macular degeneration (AMD), particularly in its advanced stages
  • Redness and irritation of the eyes are common symptoms of various conditions, including conjunctivitis (pink eye) and allergies. Inflammation of the conjunctiva can lead to a reddish appearance.
  • Photophobia or sensitivity to light is a symptom that can occur in conditions like cataracts, corneal abrasions, and inflammation of the eye.
