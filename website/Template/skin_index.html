Overview

Skin cancer, a prevalent form of cancer, often manifests through changes in moles or the development of new growths. Key indicators include asymmetry, irregular borders, varied colors, changes in size, and potential symptoms like itching, pain, or bleeding. Regular skin self-examinations and professional dermatological check-ups are critical for early detection and prompt management, helping mitigate the risks associated with skin cancer. Awareness of these signs empowers individuals to seek timely medical attention, fostering better outcomes in skin cancer prevention and treatment.

  • Skin cancer lesions often have irregular, poorly defined borders. Healthy moles typically have smooth and even edges.

  • Skin cancer may cause itching, pain, tenderness, or bleeding. Any unexplained discomfort or changes in the skin should prompt a thorough examination.
  • Skin lesions that ulcerate or crust over may be indicative of certain types of skin cancer, particularly if they persist.
