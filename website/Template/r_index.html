Oral Cancer

Overview

Oral cancer refers to the abnormal growth of cells in the oral cavity, including the lips, tongue, gums, floor of the mouth, and the palate. It encompasses various types, with squamous cell carcinoma being the most common. Early detection and intervention are crucial for successful treatment. . Oralcancer can range in seriousness from mild to life-threatening. people older than age 30-65, and people with health problems or weakened immune systems.

Symptoms

Common symptoms of oral cancer include persistent mouth sores, red or white patches, difficulty swallowing, hoarseness, lumps, and unexplained weight loss.

  • Red or white patches in the mouth.
  • The appearance of red or white patches on the tongue, gums, tonsils, or lining of the mouth, which may be accompanied by discomfort.
  • Difficulty Swallowing: Experiencing pain, discomfort, or a sensation of something stuck while swallowing (dysphagia).
  • Persistent hoarseness or a change in the voice that lasts for an extended period and is not related to a common cold or infection.
  • Lower than normal body temperature (in adults older than age 65 and people with weak immune systems)
  • Fatigue
  • The development of unexplained lumps or masses in the mouth or neck region that may be painless or associated with discomfort.
